Hi! I’m Mohammad Ali Farmani

I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate in Hydrology and Atmospheric Sciences with a minor in Data Science at the University of Arizona. My research focuses on solving complex environmental challenges by integrating physical hydrology with machine learning.

I thrive on solving real-world challenges in water and earth sciences by bridging the gap between theory and practice. Whether it’s developing machine learning-driven tools for streamflow prediction, assessing urban flood resilience, or designing databases for large-scale environmental systems, I bring a data-driven and systems-oriented approach to my work.

Key Areas of Expertise

  • Hydrological Modeling: Enhancing models like Noah-MP and RAPID for improved infiltration, runoff, and soil moisture dynamics.
  • Flood Management: Using SWMM to assess climate change impacts on urban drainage systems and improve flood resilience.
  • Database Design: Developing MySQL databases for smart water distribution and optimization systems.
  • Differentiable Hydrology: Implementing PyTorch-based hydrological processes for GPU acceleration and integration with ML models.

With proficiency in Python, R, SQL, high-performance computing, and hydrological modeling tools like SWMM, I am well-equipped to tackle interdisciplinary challenges at the nexus of water resources, climate science, and data analytics. My long-term goal is to contribute to sustainable water management and climate resilience by advancing the integration of physical and data-driven modeling approaches.

I am also passionate about collaboration and leadership, demonstrated by my roles as a committee member and website coordinator for the El Dia Student Symposium. I enjoy mentoring peers, sharing knowledge, and contributing to a culture of innovation in academia.
