Mohammad Farmani

I am a Ph.D. student in Hydrology and Atmospheric Sciences with a minor in Data Science. My research focuses on improving hydrological modeling using machine learning and physical-based models. I work extensively with the Noah-MP land surface model to analyze water movement, baseflow generation, and climate change impacts on hydrological extremes.

I have a strong background in programming, data analysis, and hydrological modeling. I use Python, Fortran, and SQL in my daily research and have experience working with high-performance computing (HPC) for large-scale hydrological simulations. My passion lies in integrating physics-based modeling with machine learning to improve predictions and enhance our understanding of water systems.
