Soil Moisture Memory (SMM) and Its Role in Land Surface Modeling

Soil Moisture Memory (SMM) refers to the duration over which soil retains the effects of a moisture perturbation, such as rainfall, before returning to its equilibrium state...

Importance of Accurate Land Models

The efficacy of LSMs in simulating climate feedback mechanisms depends critically on accurately representing soil water retention capacity and soil moisture persistence...

Key Findings from Our Study

1. Influence of Soil Hydraulic Parameterization: Brooks–Corey increases surface τL, while van Genuchten improves root zone dynamics.

2. Role of Soil Macropores: Rapid infiltration shortens surface τL and affects root zone dynamics.

3. Impact of Surface Water Ponding: Higher thresholds increase infiltration and extend τL.

Implications for Climate Science and Water Management

1. Climate Predictions and Seasonal Forecasting: Realistic SMM improves seasonal-to-subseasonal forecasts.

2. Drought and Flood Risk Management: More accurate drought/flood preparedness.

3. Agricultural and Water Resource Management: Better irrigation planning and groundwater recharge estimates.

4. Advancements in Hydrological Modeling: Highlights missing hydrologic processes like macropore infiltration.
