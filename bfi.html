Introduction to Baseflow and Streamflow Prediction

The accuracy of hydrological models in predicting streamflow, particularly in arid regions, depends on their ability to represent surface and subsurface runoff and groundwater recharge. Baseflow sustains rivers during dry periods, making its representation vital for reliable water predictions.

Importance of Understanding Baseflow Generation

Baseflow sustains streamflow during dry conditions. However, traditional LSMs often overestimate BFI due to simplified recharge and infiltration representations. Improving these processes enhances drought forecasts and water resource management.

Key Findings

1. CH model overestimates baseflow; VG captures low-BFI regions better.

2. Macropores and ponding thresholds improve recharge realism.

3. ML-calibrated parameters improved streamflow KGE by 20%.

4. IMERG forcing corrected overestimated BFI from NLDAS-2/AORC.

Implications for Climate Science and Water Management

Enhanced baseflow modeling improves drought resilience, climate forecasts, and water allocation, especially across arid basins.

Conclusion & Future Directions

Integrating improved hydraulic schemes and IMERG forcing enhanced BFI realism. The VGM + macropore configuration produced the most physically consistent outcomes for the southwestern U.S.
